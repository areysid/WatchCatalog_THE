Watch Care Tips

Proper care and maintenance can extend the life of your watch and keep it looking its best. Proper care and maintenance play a crucial role in extending the lifespan of your watch and preserving its original beauty and functionality. By implementing a consistent care routine, you not only ensure that your timepiece continues to accurately keep time but also safeguard its value and aesthetic appeal over time.
Here are some tips:

  • ➜ Regularly clean your watch with a soft cloth to remove dirt, sweat, and oils.
  • ➜ Avoid exposing your watch to extreme temperatures or direct sunlight for prolonged periods.
  • ➜ Ensure your watch is water-resistant and follow manufacturer guidelines for water exposure.
  • ➜ Keep your watch away from magnets, as they can affect the accuracy of the movement.
  • ➜ Store your watch in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and humidity to prevent damage to the case, dial, and movement.
  • ➜ Have your watch serviced by a professional watchmaker every few years to maintain its performance.
  • ➜ Store your watch in a protective case or watch box when not in use to prevent scratches and damage.
  • ➜ Adjust the time and date settings carefully to avoid damaging the crown or movement.

Glossary

Crystal: The transparent cover that protects the dial of the watch. Crystals can be made from various materials such as mineral glass, sapphire, or acrylic.
Dial: Also known as the face of the watch, the dial is the part of the watch that displays the time. It typically contains hour markers, minute markers, and hands for indicating the time.
Case: The outer housing of the watch that holds the internal components together and protects them from damage. Watch cases come in various shapes, sizes, and materials such as stainless steel, gold, or titanium.
Crown: A small knob on the side of the watch case used for setting the time, date, and other functions of the watch. It is also used to wind mechanical watches.
Bezel: The ring-shaped component surrounding the watch crystal and dial. Bezels can be stationary or rotating and are often used for measuring elapsed time or as a decorative element.
Lugs: The protruding extensions on either side of the watch case where the watch band or bracelet is attached. Lugs come in various shapes and designs and play a crucial role in securing the watch strap to the case.
Band: Also referred to as the strap or bracelet, the band is the part of the watch that wraps around the wrist. Bands can be made from leather, metal, rubber, or fabric and come in different styles such as links, mesh, or straps.
